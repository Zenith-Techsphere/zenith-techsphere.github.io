What we do

Two things, done well

We build products and we build people. Both with the same rigour.

01
Product development

We research, design, and ship micro-utility software for the Indian market — apps that solve specific, daily problems for millions of users. Our products are India-first: built around UPI, WhatsApp, joint families, and the realities of Indian digital life.

02
Internship program

We run a structured 8-week product internship for fresh graduates. Interns work in teams on real product briefs, ship working MVPs, and walk away with a live product, a GitHub portfolio, and an official certificate from a registered company — not a course completion badge.

03
AI-assisted development

Every product we build and every intern we train uses modern AI development tooling — GitHub Copilot, Bolt.new, and other tools that compress build time without cutting corners on quality. We believe the best software today is built with AI assistance, not in spite of it.

04
Validated ideas only

We do not build on hunches. Every product brief we develop is validated through community research, market signals, and first-principles analysis of real Indian pain points. If there is no clear monetisation path from day one, we do not build it.

About Zenith

A small company with a specific point of view

Zenith Techsphere Private Limited is registered in India and headquartered in Hyderabad. We are a lean team focused on one thing: building software that solves real problems for real Indians — and training the next generation of Indian product builders to do the same.


We believe the best product ideas are not glamorous. They are the ones that save someone 20 minutes a month, prevent a missed medicine dose, or help a woman running a home tiffin service get paid on time. That is the market we work in.

Ship over theorise

A working MVP with 3 users beats a perfect design with zero. We bias toward action, iteration, and real-world feedback over extended planning cycles.

India-first, always

We do not adapt Western products for India. We start from Indian behaviour — WhatsApp as infrastructure, UPI as the default, the joint family as the unit of decision-making.

Transparent by default

Whether it is our internship program fees, our IP agreements, or our selection criteria — we say what we mean and document what we say.

Build people, not just products

The internship program is not a revenue line. It is how we develop the builders who will eventually lead our products and teams.
