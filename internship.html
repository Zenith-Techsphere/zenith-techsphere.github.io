8-week curriculum

What the 8 weeks look like

Structured, week by week. No ambiguity about what you are doing or why.

Week 1
Orientation and brief reveal

Teams are formed. Product brief is shared (after onboarding agreement). Tooling is set up — GitHub Copilot, Discord, project board. Selection criteria for top 4 are announced transparently.

Week 2
Research and wireframing

User research basics, competitive analysis of the problem space, wireframing the core feature set. Tech stack decision locked in by end of week.

Weeks 3–4
MVP development

Core features built. Mentor check-in twice a week. GitHub Copilot in active use. Weekly progress shared with the full cohort.

Weeks 5–6
Build, test, and iterate

Feedback loops, QA, bug fixing, and polish. Real users may be onboarded for early testing depending on the product.

Week 7
Marketing and positioning

Landing page, social posts, and basic product positioning. You will understand not just how to build — but how to explain what you built.

Week 8
Demo Day

Each team presents their product to a live audience. Certificates and LORs are issued. Top 4 mentors announced publicly. LinkedIn posts go live.

For top performers

From intern to paid mentor in one cohort

The top 4 performers from Cohort 1 are offered a paid mentor role in Cohort 2. They lead a team of 5, receive a stipend, and carry a Mentor title at Zenith Techsphere on LinkedIn. The criteria are announced on Day 1 — no surprises, no favouritism.

Who should apply

Be honest with yourself

We are selective. Not because we want to be exclusive — but because we want a cohort that shows up.

Apply if you are
A fresh B.Tech / BCA / BSc CS graduate with 0–6 months of experience
Someone who learns by building, not by watching lectures
A person who will show up to calls, meet deadlines, and push through blockers
Looking for something concrete and verifiable to put on your CV
Comfortable with at least one of: React, Flutter, Python, or Node.js at a basic level
Do not apply if you are
Looking for a passive, self-paced learning experience
Expecting a job placement guarantee at the end
Someone who will treat this as optional or secondary to other commitments
Unwilling to work collaboratively — this is a team program, not solo
Expecting the work to be easy or the deadlines to be flexible
